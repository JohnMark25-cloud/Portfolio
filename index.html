// 01 — about

Building things that work,
then learning why they should work better.

I'm a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science graduate from the University of Antique – Tario-Lim Memorial Campus, College of Computing and Information Sciences. My path into tech started with curiosity about how websites and games are put together, and it's grown into a genuine interest in the systems underneath them — servers, networks, and the infrastructure that keeps software running at scale.

I spend most of my time across software development and web development, but I'm actively expanding into cloud computing, networking, and cybersecurity — the layers that make applications reliable and safe, not just functional. On the side, I build small games, which keeps my problem-solving sharp in ways that web projects sometimes don't.

My thesis project, Algorithm Quest, sits at the intersection of two things I care about: education and game design. It's a gamified learning platform that turns Data Structures and Algorithms — usually one of the most intimidating subjects in a CS curriculum — into something students actually want to engage with.

// 05 — projects

thesis project

Algorithm Quest

A Gamified Learning Platform for Teaching Data Structures and Algorithms

My undergraduate thesis: an educational game designed to make Data Structures and Algorithms approachable through interactive gameplay. Core DSA concepts — stacks, queues, trees, sorting, and searching — are reframed as quests and challenges, with progression mechanics that reward understanding over rote memorization. Evaluated by students, IT experts, and DSA instructors using the System Usability Scale, with results landing in the "Good" usability range across all three groups.
